# Psalm 42 Validation Report

## Test purpose

Exercise the merged v6.4 blueprint-first architecture against the approved truth-first devotional standards proposed for v7.4.

## Governing image

A deer reaching a dry water channel.

Warrant: Psalm 42:1 explicitly compares the psalmist's longing for God to a deer longing for water. The image appears in the opening, changes under the cataract language in the middle, and returns as reachable water in the closing poem.

## Lexical find

`nephesh` is rendered as "living self" to prevent the English reader from reducing the psalm's distress to a detachable spiritual compartment. The devotional applies the term to the whole breathing person without assigning a modern diagnosis.

## Exegetical truth

- The psalm presents thirst for God, remembered corporate worship, tears, hostile questioning, self-address, and repeated hope.
- The draft does not claim a known historical setting because Psalm 42 provides no certain superscription identifying one.
- The draft distinguishes explicit textual content from psychological inference.
- It does not diagnose the psalmist with depression, anxiety, trauma, or another modern category.
- The emotional movement remains unresolved longing disciplined by hope. It does not promise immediate emotional relief.

## Canonical correspondence

Classification: strong thematic echo, not direct prophecy or explicit New Testament citation.

Forward direction: the psalm's thirst is directed toward the living God and strains toward restored access to His presence.

Backward direction: John 4 and John 7 present Jesus as the giver of living water and the mediator of access to the Father by the Spirit. The devotional therefore uses fulfillment language cautiously and explicitly denies a direct citation claim.

## Psychological truth

- Faithfulness and emotional heaviness are allowed to coexist.
- Present feeling is treated as evidence of suffering, not as a complete theological verdict.
- The draft avoids universal claims that every sufferer responds identically.
- Application includes bodily rest, social disclosure, worship, and competent care without implying that devotional practice replaces clinical care.
- Waiting is not framed as payment, leverage, or a technique that guarantees relief.

## Beauty and cadence

- Reflection, Christ the Fulfillment, and Personal Application total approximately 650 words.
- No em dashes or en dashes are used.
- Prose sentences remain below 28 words.
- One primary reversal construction is retained in the Christ section.
- One main metaphor system governs the piece.
- Plain sentences follow image-bearing passages.
- A stray thread remains in the festival-cries memory and is not converted into a later symbol.

## Poem meter

Common Meter, ABCB rhyme:

- 8.6.8.6 in each stanza
- heat / Thee / feet / see
- roar / me / more / me
- tongue / near / run / fear

Light archaism appears only in the poem through "Thee."

## Judgment calls

1. The line about Christ entering apparent divine absence is theological interpretation rather than direct Psalm 42 fulfillment. The draft controls this by labeling the canonical relationship a thematic echo.
2. `nephesh` as "living self" is interpretive, but it is standard and serves the argument rather than functioning as decorative word study.
3. The final poem moves from dry channels to clear running water. This is a canonical transformation through Christ, not a claim that Psalm 42 itself resolves every emotional tension immediately.

Pass for manual truth, psychological plausibility, literary structure, and read-aloud review.
