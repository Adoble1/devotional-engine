# Taste and Narrative Contract

This contract incorporates the non-redundant parts of the proposed Devotional Engine v8 taste system into the integrated production path.

## Added to the production contract

### One lexical insight

The grounder must provide one standard Hebrew, Aramaic, or Greek observation with:

- the source language;
- the term;
- the observation;
- the role the observation plays in the devotional argument.

The composer receives the insight but is told to use it only when it advances the argument. A decorative word study is a failure of judgment.

### Warranted governing-image selection

The planner must generate at least three candidates. Each candidate records:

- the concrete image;
- textual or verified narrative warrant;
- sensory grain;
- a transformation path across the devotional;
- a note on novelty against the supplied series ledger.

The selected image must come from that set, and the planner must explain the choice. The composer receives only the selected design and concise continuity metadata, not the full research machinery.

### Narrative depth by warrant

The planner chooses one of three narrative levels:

1. dramatized speaker within the textual situation;
2. one bounded scene supported by a superscription or clear canonical event;
3. parallel biblical scenes when the Christological mechanism is itself narrative.

Level 2 or 3 requires a source basis and a bounded-scene statement. Narrative vividness never licenses invented motives, weather, institutions, named people, or private emotion.

### Human-cadence review

The literary audit now advises on:

- dependence on em or en dashes for sentence structure;
- redundant intensifiers;
- repeated balanced reversals;
- unusually uniform sentence lengths;
- consecutive paragraphs with the same opening footprint.

These findings remain advisory. They inform the reviewer and targeted revision without becoming universal hard bans.

## Preserved as local series choices

The following proposals are useful for some series but are not global engine laws:

- a fixed 500 to 700 word range;
- mandatory Common Meter and one rhyme scheme;
- an exact heading template with a Historical Setting section;
- mandatory use of named commentators;
- a universal ban on every dash or every rhetorical triad;
- one required devotional tone or narrative intensity.

The current engine keeps poem form, section weight, and surface rhythm inside protected aesthetic freedom unless a project or series profile explicitly configures them.

## Scripture wording

The v8 phrase `Working translation from the Masoretic Text` is not adopted as an automatic label. The existing Scripture provenance contract remains authoritative. Independent renderings must identify their Hebrew or Greek source and remain marked for human review before publication.
